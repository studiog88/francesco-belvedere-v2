Product Design

Unlocking the creative spark in each of us.

Figment is a daily creativity app that helps people build an expressive habit through short exercises in drawing, music, poetry, and more.

01 — THE BRIEF

A new product bet for Lumosity

Lumosity wanted to test whether their brain-training audience would engage with creative tools. Figment was the answer — an end-to-end MVP designed and shipped in about five months. I first joined the brand team to help with naming and visual identity, then moved to the product team to lead the UX and UI.

02 — THE CHALLENGE

Twelve activity types. One coherent experience.

Each exercise — drawing, music composition, poetry, collage, tangrams, and more — had different goals and required different inputs and constraints without being cluttered and over-instructed. My job was to condense the experience for each exercise into something that felt inviting and attainable in under five minutes, while keeping the whole app feeling like a single product.

03 — THE APPROACH

One shared frame with unique individual canvases.

I designed a consistent structural shell — navigation, entry/exit points, design system with UI patterns and components — and let each exercise own the creative space inside it. Users move between activities without ever feeling lost. The entire app was built entirely in Unity, which freed the UI from native constraints and let the interface itself feel more expressive of the brand.

04 — BRAND & DESIGN SYSTEM

A visual identity that lives inside the product

Previous to the UI work and before transitioning to the product role, I was part of the brand team that created Figment's name, logo, and visual identity. It was imperative that the visual continuity showed through in the UI. I pushed to expresses the brand through color, typography, illustration, and motion, not just as a skin on top of components, but throughout the entire app and the way it interacted with the user.

05 — THE DETAILS

Daily themes, community, dark mode

A daily-theme system gave users a reason to come back without the paradox of choice. A community gallery let people share creations anonymously and see how others interpreted the same prompt. Light and dark modes offered different emotional registers for different moments.

07 — OUTCOME

You can still find it in the App Store

Figment launched to five-star reviews from users who called it the first app of its kind. It remains available in the App Store years after release. A shift in company priorities eventually moved focus back to Lumosity's core product, but the work endures — and it was the first project at Lumosity where I carried a product from brand identity through to shipped UI on a single arc.
